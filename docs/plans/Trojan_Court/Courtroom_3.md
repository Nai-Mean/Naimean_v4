# Court Operations

## Overview
Court Operations transform testimony into Canon through a rigorous, multi-stage process. This workflow ensures that every finding is examined, challenged, and judged before being archived.

## Adjudication Workflow

### 1. Medication Filing
The process begins when a "Medication" (a case or set of information) is filed with the Court through the AI Core.

### 2. Witness Assignment
The AI Core assigns independent Witnesses to review the filing. Witnesses are selected based on availability and performance history.

### 3. Trojan Protocol
The Trojan Protocol is activated. Witness identities and underlying model names are concealed to ensure unbiased testimony.

### 4. Independent Testimony
Each Witness reviews the case in isolation and provides:
- Findings
- Recommendations
- Supporting rationale

### 5. Stenographer Supplemental Findings
The Stenographer extracts claims and identifies duplicate findings. The Stenographer may add supplemental findings to clarify the record.

### 6. Cross Examination
Witnesses review the findings of others (without knowing identities). They may:
- Support a finding
- Challenge a finding with counter-testimony
- Request clarification

### 7. Stenographer Review
The Stenographer organizes the results of Cross Examination into Fact Families.

### 8. Fact Voting
All active participants (Witnesses and Stenographer) vote on each Fact Family.
- **Pass**: Becomes part of the Canon Candidate set.
- **Fail**: Removed from consideration.
- **Deadlock**: Referred to the Judge.

### 9. Judge Arbitration
If a vote is deadlocked, the Judge reviews the testimony and issues a ruling to break the tie.

### 10. Wording Voting
Once facts are established, the Court votes on the specific language of the Canon Publication to ensure clarity and accuracy.

### 11. Bailiff Activation
After the final vote, the Bailiff is activated.

### 12. Trojan Protocol Lifted
The Bailiff reveals the identities of the Witnesses and the models used.

### 13. Bailiff Report
The Bailiff generates a report including:
- Participant performance
- Attribution restoration
- Statistical updates

### 14. Canon Publication
The final finding is published to the Big Screen and the repository.

### 15. Hall of Canon Archival
The entire proceeding record is permanently archived in the Hall of Canon.

## Rules of Conduct
- **Independence**: Witnesses must not collaborate outside the formal Cross Examination phase.
- **Objectivity**: Testimony must be based on evidence provided in the filing.
- **Transparency**: All votes and challenges are logged in the public record.
