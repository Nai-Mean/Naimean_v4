(Plan) Medication Canon

Purpose

The Medication Canon is the official output produced by the Trojan Courthouse during Phase 1 proceedings.

The Medication Canon is optimized for rapid clinical review within inpatient substance use disorder treatment, behavioral health, detoxification, and residential rehabilitation environments.

The objective is not to create a traditional drug monograph.

The objective is to provide actionable clinical guidance that has survived testimony, cross examination, voting, arbitration, and Canon review.

⸻

Intended Audience

Primary users include:

* Nurses
* Providers
* Pharmacists
* Clinical Informaticists
* Behavioral Health Staff

The Medication Canon prioritizes:

* Patient Safety
* Clinical Utility
* Actionability
* Readability
* Risk Recognition

The Court is designed to support inpatient treatment environments rather than retail pharmacy counseling.

⸻

Medication Canon Structure

Every medication proceeding produces Canon using the following structure.

⸻

Header Format

Example:

Ativan (Lorazepam) : Benzodiazepine
Schedule IV : Lower abuse and dependence risk
Pregnancy Category D : Positive evidence of fetal risk

For non-scheduled medications:

Vistaril (Hydroxyzine) : First-Generation Antihistamine / Anxiolytic
Pregnancy Category C : Risk cannot be ruled out

Schedule classification is displayed only when applicable.

⸻

Patient Recommendations

Patient Recommendations provide practical guidance relevant to medication use.

Examples:

* Common expectations
* Activity recommendations
* Timing considerations
* Administration considerations
* Important precautions
* Adherence guidance

Recommendations should reflect supervised inpatient treatment environments whenever appropriate.

⸻

Most Common Side Effects

The Court identifies the side effects most likely to be encountered during routine use.

Preference is given to:

* Clinically significant effects
* Commonly observed effects
* Patient-relevant effects

The objective is to prepare clinicians and patients for expected outcomes.

⸻

Most Dangerous Warning Signs

The Court identifies adverse findings requiring immediate attention.

Preference is given to:

* Life-threatening complications
* Serious adverse reactions
* Escalation triggers
* High-risk presentations

The objective is early recognition and intervention.

⸻

RCA Guidelines

RCA Guidelines contain organization-specific guidance relevant to the medication.

Examples may include:

* Internal workflows
* Preferred practices
* Organizational standards
* Local protocols

This section is intentionally separated from clinical facts.

Clinical facts remain portable.

Organizational guidance remains local.

⸻

Mechanism of Action

The Court explains how the medication works.

The explanation should remain concise.

The objective is clinical understanding rather than academic detail.

Examples:

* Receptor activity
* Neurotransmitter effects
* Physiologic actions
* Therapeutic effects

Mechanism explanations should remain understandable to clinicians outside specialty practice areas.

⸻

Nursing Considerations

Nursing Considerations focus on bedside assessment and patient observation.

⸻

Nursing Monitoring

Nursing Monitoring includes findings that should be routinely observed.

Examples:

* Sedation
* Mental status changes
* Respiratory depression
* Fall risk
* Withdrawal symptoms
* Adverse effects

Monitoring recommendations should emphasize actionable observations.

⸻

Escalation Criteria

Escalation Criteria identify findings requiring provider notification or intervention.

Examples:

* Significant oversedation
* Respiratory compromise
* Severe adverse reactions
* New neurologic findings
* Suspected toxicity

The objective is to support early intervention.

⸻

Ordering Considerations

Ordering Considerations focus on safe prescribing and medication management.

⸻

Contraindications

Contraindications identify situations where a medication should not be used or should be used with extreme caution.

Examples:

* Absolute contraindications
* Relative contraindications
* Black Box Warnings
* High-risk interactions
* Pregnancy concerns
* Severe organ dysfunction

⸻

Laboratory Monitoring

Laboratory Monitoring identifies testing that may be necessary before or during treatment.

Examples:

* Renal function
* Hepatic function
* Drug levels
* Complete blood count
* Electrolytes
* Endocrine monitoring

Monitoring recommendations should be practical and clinically relevant.

⸻

Metabolism / Elimination

The Court summarizes how the medication is processed by the body.

⸻

Primary Metabolism

Examples:

* Liver
* Plasma
* Minimal metabolism

⸻

Major Enzymes

Examples:

* CYP3A4
* CYP2D6
* CYP2C19

Only clinically meaningful enzymes should be included.

⸻

Primary Elimination

Examples:

* Renal
* Hepatic
* Mixed

⸻

Clinically Important Interactions

The Court identifies interactions likely to influence safety or effectiveness.

Examples:

* CYP inhibitors
* CYP inducers
* CNS depressants
* QT-prolonging medications

The objective is practical risk recognition.

⸻

Reasons For Use

The Court distinguishes between approved indications and common off-label use.

⸻

FDA Approved Uses

FDA Approved Uses contain labeled indications supported by regulatory approval.

Examples:

* Anxiety
* Hypertension
* Seizure Disorder
* Depression

⸻

Common Off-Label Uses

Common Off-Label Uses contain indications commonly encountered in clinical practice that are not part of FDA-approved labeling.

Examples:

* Sleep Support
* Neuropathic Pain
* Withdrawal Support
* Anxiety Adjunct Therapy

Off-label uses must always be identified as off-label.

The Court must never present an off-label indication as an FDA-approved indication.

⸻

Canon Confidence Score

Every accepted Canon finding receives a Confidence Score.

The Confidence Score reflects:

* Vote Strength
* Agreement Level
* Safety Priority
* Clinical Priority
* Judge Involvement

Examples:

Monitor QTc interval
Confidence: 96%
Accepted 4-0
Monitor hepatic enzymes
Confidence: 64%
Accepted after Judge Arbitration

The purpose of the Confidence Score is to help clinicians rapidly distinguish strong consensus findings from findings requiring additional scrutiny.

⸻

Canonical Language Selection

The Court distinguishes between:

* Fact Accuracy
* Language Quality

A fact may be accepted while its wording is rejected.

The Court first determines whether a fact is valid.

Only after a fact is accepted does the Court determine which wording best represents that fact.

The Court does not rewrite findings.

The Court selects from wording already present in testimony.

⸻

Canon Wording Evaluation

The objective of Canon wording is clinical usefulness.

The objective is not literary quality.

The objective is not verbosity.

The objective is actionable communication.

⸻

Clinical Accuracy

Weight: 35%

Question:

Does the wording preserve the original medical meaning?

Considerations:

* No loss of meaning
* No unsupported additions
* No overstatement
* No understatement

⸻

Safety Impact

Weight: 25%

Question:

Does the wording clearly communicate patient safety concerns?

Preference is given to wording that:

* Prevents harm
* Encourages monitoring
* Identifies escalation criteria
* Improves recognition of adverse effects

⸻

Actionability

Weight: 15%

Question:

Does the wording clearly tell the reader what action should be taken?

Example:

Weak:

QTc prolongation may occur.

Strong:

Monitor QTc interval and notify the provider of significant prolongation.

⸻

Clinical Relevance

Weight: 10%

Question:

Is this wording useful within an inpatient treatment environment?

Preference is given to wording directly useful to:

* Nursing
* Pharmacy
* Providers
* Clinical Operations

⸻

Clarity

Weight: 10%

Question:

Can a clinician immediately understand the recommendation?

Preference is given to:

* Minimal ambiguity
* Easy interpretation
* Direct communication

⸻

Efficiency

Weight: 5%

Question:

Does the wording communicate maximum value with minimal unnecessary language?

Preference is given to:

* Concise wording
* High information density
* Minimal redundancy

⸻

Medication Canon Doctrine

The Medication Canon is the final clinical output of the Court.

Every finding within Canon has survived:

* Independent Testimony
* Cross Examination
* Fact Voting
* Wording Voting
* Judicial Review (if required)

The Court does not generate truth.

The Court examines truth.

The Court challenges truth.

The Court judges truth.

Only then does truth become Canon.
