AI Core, Configuration, Deployment, and Operations

Purpose

The AI Core is the operational engine beneath the Trojan Courthouse.

The courthouse serves as the public interface.

The AI Core serves as the administrative and technical control center.

The AI Core administers proceedings.

The AI Core does not influence outcomes.

Court Operations and AI Core Operations remain intentionally separated.

⸻

AI Core Overview

The AI Core is located beneath the Trojan Courthouse.

Access is provided through the Judge’s Stand hotspot.

The AI Core serves as the administrative center for:

* Model Management
* Court Configuration
* Authentication
* API Management
* Statistics
* Hall of Canon Administration
* System Monitoring
* Deployment Operations

The AI Core is not visible during active proceedings.

Case Qualification

Not every prompt automatically qualifies as a valid proceeding.

Before testimony begins, Odysseus evaluates whether sufficient information exists to support meaningful examination.

Examples of weak filings:

* Help me code
* Fix this
* Tell me about databases
* What’s best?

These filings lack sufficient context.

The Court requests clarification when necessary.

Examples of requested information:

* Desired Outcome
* Constraints
* Environment
* Existing Implementation
* Supporting Documentation
* Scope

Proceedings begin only after enough information exists to support meaningful analysis.

⸻

Insufficient Case Record

If a filing remains insufficient after clarification attempts, the Court shall decline to convene.

A proceeding requires enough information to support meaningful testimony.

When sufficient context cannot be established, Odysseus enters Fallback Mode.

⸻

Fallback Mode

The user is presented with available models.

The user may select a model directly.

Examples:

* GPT
* Gemini
* Claude
* MedGemma
* Ollama Models

The selected model receives the prompt without Court adjudication.

The response is displayed directly on the Big Screen.

No witnesses are seated.

No cross examination occurs.

No voting occurs.

No Canon is produced.

No Hall of Canon record is created.

The response represents the selected model’s opinion rather than a Court ruling.

⸻

Garbage In, Garbage Out Doctrine

The Court cannot reliably establish Canon from incomplete information.

Weak prompts increase the likelihood of:

* Assumptions
* Hallucinations
* Ambiguous Findings
* Low-Confidence Outcomes

When necessary, the Court shall request clarification before testimony begins.

The objective is not to answer immediately.

The objective is to establish useful Canon.

Proceedings may be delayed to improve the quality of the case record.

If a sufficient case record cannot be established, the Court shall defer to direct model interaction rather than produce low-quality Canon.

The Court prefers delayed adjudication over low-quality Canon.
