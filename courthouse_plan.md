# Overview

The Trojan Courthouse is the primary adjudication system of the Naimean ecosystem.

Unlike traditional AI systems that rely upon a single model and a single response, the Trojan Courthouse treats every prompt as a formal case.

Cases are filed.

Witnesses testify.

Witnesses challenge one another.

Arguments are examined.

Judgment is rendered.

The Bailiff certifies the proceeding.

The final Canon is displayed.

The complete record is archived.

The objective is not merely to generate answers.

The objective is to establish trustworthy Canon through transparent examination.

Primary Interface:

```text
https://naimean.com/courtroom
```

## The Stenographer

The Stenographer is a preservation and annotation system.

The Stenographer never:

- Deletes findings
- Merges findings
- Rewrites findings
- Determines truth

Responsibilities:

- Preserve testimony
- Preserve revisions
- Preserve challenges
- Preserve votes
- Assign claim identifiers
- Hide witness identities
- Annotate findings
- Prepare Judge transcript

Every claim survives.

Nothing is lost.

## The Judge

The Judge reviews anonymized testimony.

The Judge never knows:

- Model identities
- Witness identities
- Witness performance

The Judge evaluates arguments.

Not sources.

Responsibilities:

- Resolve disputes
- Accept findings
- Reject findings
- Select Canonical Language
- Render judgment

### Canonical Language Selection

When multiple findings communicate the same idea, the Judge selects:

- Most accurate wording
- Most intuitive wording
- Most actionable wording
- Most clinically useful wording

Primary audience:

- Nurses
- Providers
- Clinical Informaticists
- Healthcare Leadership
- Technical Professionals

Goal:

```text
Best Fact
+
Best Wording
=
Canonical Finding
```

---

## Witness Testimony

Three witnesses independently review the case.

Witnesses do not initially see one another's findings.

Each witness:

- Analyzes the case
- Produces testimony
- Lists findings
- Identifies uncertainty
- Provides recommendations

---

## Cross Examination

Each witness receives:

- Their own testimony
- Other witness testimony

Witnesses must:

- Defend valid conclusions
- Retract weak conclusions
- Adopt stronger evidence
- Challenge unsupported claims

The result is revised testimony.

Many hallucinations are removed before judgment occurs.

---

## Blind Judgment

The Judge receives anonymized findings only.

Example:

```text
Claim C-014

Supported By: 3 Witnesses

Disputed By: 1 Witness
```

The Judge evaluates claims.

Not identities.
