Court Operations

Purpose

Court Operations define how a case moves through the Trojan Courthouse.

Every proceeding follows the same lifecycle regardless of subject matter.

The objective is to transform testimony into Canon through examination, challenge, voting, and arbitration.

The Court does not assume truth.

The Court investigates truth.

⸻

Filing

Every proceeding begins with a filing.

A filing contains the question, issue, medication, problem, or topic to be examined.

Examples:

* Vistaril 50 mg
* Lorazepam 1 mg
* Compare React and Vue
* Design a database schema
* Analyze a treatment protocol

The filing becomes the official case record.

⸻

Case Qualification

Not every filing automatically qualifies as a valid court proceeding.

Before testimony begins, Odysseus evaluates whether enough information exists to support meaningful examination.

Examples of insufficient filings:

* Help me code
* Fix this
* Tell me about databases
* What’s best?

These filings lack sufficient context.

The Court requests clarification when necessary.

Examples:

* Desired outcome
* Environment
* Constraints
* Existing implementation
* Supporting documentation
* Scope

The Court prefers delayed adjudication over low-quality Canon.

⸻

Fallback Mode

If a filing remains insufficient after clarification attempts, the Court shall decline to convene.

Instead, the user may select a model directly.

Examples:

* GPT
* Gemini
* Claude
* MedGemma
* Local Ollama Models

The selected model answers the question directly.

The response is displayed on the Big Screen.

No witnesses are seated.

No voting occurs.

No Canon is generated.

No Hall of Canon record is created.

The response represents the opinion of a single model rather than a Court ruling.

⸻

Witness Assignment

Once a filing qualifies, the Court convenes.

Three witnesses are selected.

Witnesses are chosen randomly from eligible models.

Court identities are assigned.

Example:

Witness A : TARS

Witness B : ORACLE

Witness C : T-1000

Underlying model identities remain hidden.

The Trojan Protocol is activated.

⸻

Trojan Protocol

The Trojan Protocol preserves blind adjudication.

Participants evaluate findings rather than model reputation.

During active proceedings:

* Witness identities remain concealed
* Model identities remain concealed
* Historical performance remains concealed

Participants see only Court identities.

Examples:

* TARS
* ORACLE
* T-1000

The purpose is to eliminate model bias.

⸻

Independent Testimony

Each witness initially reviews the filing in isolation.

Witnesses do not see:

* Other testimony
* Other findings
* Other recommendations

Witnesses produce independent testimony.

Each witness may provide:

* Findings
* Recommendations
* Risks
* Supporting rationale
* Warnings
* Alternative viewpoints

This phase maximizes independent discovery.

⸻

Cross Examination

Cross Examination begins after testimony is complete.

Witnesses receive:

* Their own testimony
* Other witness testimony

Witnesses are expected to:

* Defend findings
* Challenge findings
* Clarify reasoning
* Correct mistakes
* Adopt stronger evidence
* Retract weak positions

The objective is scrutiny.

The objective is not agreement.

Cross Examination exists to reduce hallucinations and strengthen findings before voting begins.

⸻

Stenographer Review

The Stenographer reviews the completed proceeding.

The Stenographer remains blinded by the Trojan Protocol.

The Stenographer does not know:

* Witness identities
* Underlying models
* Historical performance

The Stenographer extracts all claims.

Nothing is deleted.

Nothing is merged.

Nothing is rewritten.

Every claim is preserved.

⸻

Claim Extraction

Each meaningful statement receives a Claim ID.

Examples:

C-001

C-002

C-003

Claims remain traceable throughout the proceeding.

The Court never loses the original testimony.

⸻

Fact Families

The Stenographer groups related claims into Fact Families.

Example:

Claim A

Monitor for worsening withdrawal symptoms.

Claim B

Assess for worsening withdrawal symptoms and notify staff.

Claim C

Observe for symptom escalation.

These may belong to:

Fact Family F-014

The underlying fact is grouped.

The original wording remains preserved.

⸻

Fact Voting

The Court first evaluates facts.

Question:

Is the underlying fact valid?

Voting Participants:

* Witness A
* Witness B
* Witness C
* Stenographer

Possible Outcomes:

4-0 Accept

3-1 Accept

1-3 Reject

0-4 Reject

Consensus outcomes do not require Judge review.

⸻

Deadlocks

Deadlock occurs when voting results in:

2-2

Deadlocks are escalated to the Judge.

The Judge does not review consensus decisions.

The Judge reviews only unresolved disputes.

⸻

Wording Voting

Once a fact is accepted, the Court evaluates wording.

Question:

Which wording best represents the accepted fact?

The Court does not rewrite findings.

The Court selects from existing testimony.

The wording receiving the highest support becomes Canon wording.

Tied wording selections are escalated to the Judge.

⸻

Canon Wording Evaluation

Wording candidates are scored according to:

Clinical Accuracy

Safety Impact

Actionability

Clinical Relevance

Clarity

Efficiency

The objective is to produce the most clinically useful wording rather than the most verbose wording.

⸻

Stenographer Vote

The Stenographer participates in voting.

The Stenographer is not a witness.

The Stenographer serves as an independent medical reviewer.

The fourth vote exists for two reasons:

* Independent review
* Deadlock creation

Without a fourth vote, judicial arbitration would rarely occur.

⸻

Judge Arbitration

The Judge serves as the Court’s final arbiter.

The Judge does not:

* Create findings
* Modify findings
* Delete findings
* Participate in testimony

The Judge resolves:

* Fact Deadlocks
* Wording Deadlocks

The Judge reviews only unresolved disputes.

Consensus belongs to the Court.

Deadlocks belong to the Judge.

⸻

Bailiff Activation

The Bailiff remains inactive throughout adjudication.

The Bailiff becomes active only after proceedings conclude.

The Bailiff receives:

* Voting Results
* Judge Rulings
* Canon Selections
* Attribution Mapping

The Bailiff does not influence outcomes.

⸻

Trojan Protocol Lifted

After adjudication concludes:

Trojan Protocol Lifted

Attribution is restored.

Example:

Witness A : TARS : GPT-5

Witness B : ORACLE : Gemini 2.5 Pro

Witness C : T-1000 : MedGemma

Judge : ATHENA : Claude Opus

Stenographer : DATA : MedGemma

Bailiff : JANUS : GPT-5 Mini

⸻

Bailiff Report

The Bailiff prepares the official ruling.

The report contains:

* Accepted Facts
* Rejected Facts
* Judge Decisions
* Canon Wording
* Performance Updates
* Attribution

The Bailiff announces the outcome of the proceeding.

⸻

Canon Publication

Accepted findings become Canon.

Canon is the official output of the Court.

Canon is published to:

* The Big Screen
* The Hall of Canon
* Statistics Systems

Rejected findings remain preserved within the case record.

⸻

Hall of Canon Archival

Every proceeding is archived.

Nothing is deleted.

Everything survives.

Archived materials include:

* Filing
* Testimony
* Cross Examination
* Fact Families
* Voting Records
* Judge Rulings
* Bailiff Reports
* Canon

The Hall of Canon serves as the permanent institutional memory of Odysseus.

⸻

Performance Doctrine

Agent performance is recorded.

Agent performance is trended.

Agent performance is archived.

Performance does not influence voting authority.

Performance does not influence witness selection.

Performance does not influence judicial authority.

Every proceeding begins with equal authority.

Performance determines reputation.

Performance does not determine power.
